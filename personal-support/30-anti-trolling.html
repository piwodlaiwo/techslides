How to Use AI to Deal with Online Trolling

Anyone who's spent time on social media knows the particular exhaustion of dealing with bad-faith comments, harassment, or pile-ons — and AI has become a useful tool for navigating that landscape without losing your cool or your time. People use it to draft measured, confident responses to trolling that don't escalate the situation, to think through whether something is worth responding to at all, and to process the emotional sting of online hostility before deciding what to do. AI is also useful for writing clear, firm statements if you need to publicly address misinformation or set a boundary. The practical approach: paste in the offending comment, explain the context, and ask AI to help you respond in a way that's calm, clear, and doesn't give the troll what they want.

5 Best Prompts for Dealing with Online Trolling to Ask Claude or ChatGPT

Copy any prompt below and paste it directly into your AI of choice.

  1. Prompt 01 · Draft a response

    "Someone posted this comment directed at me: [paste comment]. I want to respond in a way that's calm, confident, and doesn't escalate — but also doesn't roll over. Can you help me draft a response that shuts it down without giving them the reaction they want?"

    Best for: responding to trolling with composure rather than emotion.

  2. Prompt 02 · Decide whether to engage

    "Here's a comment I received: [paste comment]. I'm torn between ignoring it and responding. Can you help me think through: is this worth engaging with, what do I lose or gain by responding, and what does the person likely want from this interaction?"

    Best for: the often-overlooked question of whether to engage at all.

  3. Prompt 03 · Process the sting

    "I know rationally that this comment shouldn't bother me, but it does: [paste comment]. Can you help me understand why it landed and how to think about it more clearly, so I can let it go rather than stewing on it?"

    Best for: processing the emotional impact of online hostility before deciding what to do.

  4. Prompt 04 · Address misinformation publicly

    "Someone is spreading false information about me/my work: [describe]. I want to respond publicly in a way that corrects the record clearly without sounding defensive or petty. Can you help me write something that's factual, confident, and brief?"

    Best for: setting the record straight without getting drawn into a mud fight.

  5. Prompt 05 · Set a firm boundary

    "Someone keeps [describe behavior: sending hostile messages, tagging me in negative posts, leaving repeated negative comments]. I want to send them one clear message telling them to stop, before I block/report them. Can you help me write something firm, clear, and unemotional?"

    Best for: drawing a line once, clearly, before cutting off contact.
